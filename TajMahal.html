The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most breathtaking monuments in the world. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, it is a symbol of eternal love and devotion. Its white marble domes and minarets reflect the finest example of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles.

Highlights

  • Constructed between 1632 and 1648, employing over 20,000 artisans.
  • The central dome rises to 73 meters and is surrounded by four elegant minarets.
  • Decorated with precious stones, intricate carvings, and Quranic inscriptions.
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Visitor Experience

Visitors can stroll through the lush gardens, admire the reflection of the Taj Mahal in the central pool, and marvel at its stunning symmetry. At sunrise and sunset, the marble shifts colors from pink to golden, creating a mesmerizing experience. The Taj Mahal remains a timeless masterpiece and a tribute to love.
