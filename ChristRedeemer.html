Standing high atop Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Christ the Redeemer is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world. This colossal Art Deco statue symbolizes peace, faith, and the warm spirit of Brazil. At 30 meters tall with outstretched arms spanning 28 meters, the statue embraces the vibrant city below.

Highlights

  • Completed in 1931 and designed by French sculptor Paul Landowski.
  • Recognized as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World in 2007.
  • Offers panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro, Sugarloaf Mountain, and Copacabana Beach.
  • A spiritual symbol visited by millions each year.

Visitor Experience

A trip to Christ the Redeemer is both a spiritual and scenic journey. Visitors can reach the summit by train or van and enjoy breathtaking views of Rio's coastline and lush forests. At sunset, the statue is bathed in golden light, creating an unforgettable sight.
