The Great Pyramid of Giza, built for the Pharaoh Khufu around 2570 BCE, is the oldest and only surviving member of the original Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Its astonishing precision, monumental scale, and enduring mystery continue to inspire engineers, historians, and travelers alike.

Highlights

  • Constructed from ~2.3 million limestone blocks.
  • Originally stood ~146.6 m; today ~138.8 m due to loss of casing stones.

Explore the Giza Plateau, home to the Great Pyramid, the Pyramid of Khafre, the Pyramid of Menkaure, and the Great Sphinx. Wander the causeways, visit the Solar Boat Museum (if open during your visit), and experience the evening sound and light show that illuminates the ancient monuments under the desert sky.
